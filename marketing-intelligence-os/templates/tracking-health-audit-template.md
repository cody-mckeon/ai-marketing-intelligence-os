# Tracking Health Audit

## 1. Tracking Scope

Summarize the page, campaign, initiative, user actions, platforms, and reporting questions covered by this audit.

## 2. Required Tracking Inventory

List the events, CTAs, UTMs, platforms, and conversion paths that should exist for the report to be trustworthy.

## 3. Available Tracking Evidence

Document the tracking evidence actually provided, such as GA4 events, Pendo features, GTM tags, UTM docs, paid media reports, vendor reports, or manual QA notes. If none was provided, state that clearly.

## 4. Missing or Unconfirmed Tracking

List the missing, incomplete, or unconfirmed tracking items that must be validated before reporting.

## 5. Event and CTA Validation Needs

Identify the CTA clicks, events, parameters, destinations, and deduplication rules that need to be checked.

## 6. Campaign and UTM Health

Assess whether campaign tracking appears ready, missing, inconsistent, or unvalidated based on the available UTM and campaign evidence.

## 7. Consent, Attribution, and Platform Caveats

Call out likely caveats related to consent behavior, attribution, cross-domain tracking, internal traffic, reporting windows, and platform mismatch.

## 8. Third-Party and Vendor Visibility

Assess visibility into vendor or third-party outcomes, clearly separating onsite intent from completed bookings, reservations, ticket purchases, form submissions, or revenue.

## 9. Reporting Readiness Assessment

Provide one readiness status and a short reason: Ready for Leadership Reporting, Ready with Caveats, Not Ready - Validation Needed, or Not Ready - Missing Data.

## 10. Recommended Next Actions

List the practical validation steps or data requests needed to improve tracking confidence or move toward reporting.
