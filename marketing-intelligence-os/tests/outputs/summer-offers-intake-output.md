# Analytics Request Brief

## 1. Request Summary

- Brief summary: A stakeholder wants a leadership-ready performance brief for the Summer Offers page, which launched in March and includes hotel, dining, and entertainment offers. The request needs to cover traffic, engagement, CTA behavior, campaign performance, and whether users showed booking intent.
- Request type: Mixed Request
- Included request types, if mixed:
  - Page Performance
  - Campaign Performance
  - Leadership Reporting
  - Conversion / Intent Analysis
  - Vendor / Third-Party Reporting

## 2. Primary Business Question

Did the Summer Offers page drive qualified engagement and booking-intent actions across hotel, dining, and entertainment offers?

## 3. Reporting Audience

The primary audience is marketing leadership and website/product stakeholders who need a concise, leadership-ready readout. Campaign owners, channel managers, and agency partners may need supporting detail for traffic source and UTM performance.

## 4. Decision This Report Supports

This report should help the team decide whether the Summer Offers page and related campaigns are generating meaningful interest, which offer categories are most engaging, where traffic quality is strongest, and what optimizations or follow-up reporting should happen next.

## 5. Recommended Metrics

- Sessions
- Users
- Engaged sessions
- Engagement rate
- Average engagement time
- Traffic source / medium
- UTM campaign performance
- Landing page sessions
- CTA clicks
- Offer/category engagement
- Booking intent clicks
- Reservation clicks
- Ticket clicks
- Scroll depth
- Device category
- Mobile vs desktop behavior
- Exit behavior

## 6. Likely Data Sources

- GA4 for page traffic, engagement, source / medium, campaign / UTM reporting, device behavior, and event activity
- Pendo for product or onsite behavior if the Summer Offers experience is tracked there
- GTM for validating CTA, scroll, booking intent, reservation, and ticket click event definitions
- Booking engine for hotel booking flow or booking-intent destination data
- SevenRooms for dining reservation clickthrough or reservation data, if available
- AXS / Fevo for entertainment ticket clickthrough or ticketing data, if available
- Paid media reports for campaign delivery, clicks, spend, and channel-reported performance
- Campaign UTM documentation for campaign naming, source / medium rules, and launch context

## 7. Required Inputs

- Exact reporting date range, including whether analysis should start on the March launch date or a later campaign start date
- Summer Offers page URL and any related landing page variants
- Campaign names, UTM parameters, and channel taxonomy
- List of hotel, dining, and entertainment offers included on the page
- CTA and event definitions for booking intent, reservation clicks, ticket clicks, and offer/category engagement
- Confirmation of conversion destinations, including booking engine, SevenRooms, AXS, Fevo, or other third-party platforms
- Paid media or agency reports for the same reporting period
- Leadership reporting expectations, including whether the output should be a brief, slide outline, or narrative summary

## 8. Known Data Caveats

- Third-party booking, reservation, and ticketing platforms may limit full-funnel visibility from page visit to completed revenue.
- CTA clicks and booking-intent clicks indicate intent, not completed bookings, reservations, ticket purchases, or revenue.
- UTM quality may affect campaign reporting if naming is inconsistent or missing.
- Consent settings may reduce observable tracking.
- GA4 and ad platform numbers may not match because of attribution, click/session definitions, consent, and reporting windows.
- Internal traffic may pollute results if filters are incomplete.
- Some events, such as offer/category engagement or scroll depth, may require GTM validation before they can be trusted.
- SevenRooms, AXS, Fevo, or booking engine data may require vendor access or support.

## 9. Suggested Reporting Format

- Executive summary
- Traffic overview
- Campaign performance
- Offer/category engagement
- CTA and booking-intent behavior
- Device behavior
- Data quality notes
- Recommended next actions

Run the Page Performance Agent first to evaluate the Summer Offers page traffic, engagement, CTA behavior, and offer/category interactions.

After the page performance brief is complete, run the Leadership Insight Agent to turn the findings, caveats, and recommended next actions into a leadership-ready narrative.
