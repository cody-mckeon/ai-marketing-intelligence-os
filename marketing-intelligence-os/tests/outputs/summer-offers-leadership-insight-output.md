## 4. Why It Matters

The Summer Offers page is intended to help visitors discover seasonal hotel, dining, and entertainment offers and move toward the right booking, reservation, or ticketing path. For leadership, the important question is not only whether the page received traffic, but whether that traffic appeared qualified and whether users took actions that suggest commercial intent.

A strong final readout should help the team understand which offers and channels deserve more investment, where the user journey may need optimization, and whether the measurement setup is strong enough to support confident campaign and conversion reporting.

Because many final actions may happen in third-party platforms, the business interpretation needs to separate onsite engagement and intent from completed conversion or revenue.

## 5. Key Signals

The final leadership readout should focus on these signals:

- Traffic quality: Sessions, engaged sessions, engagement rate, average engagement time, exit behavior, and new versus returning users
- Campaign quality: Source / medium and UTM performance by campaign, channel, content, and term where available
- Qualified engagement: Scroll depth, offer card clicks, category interaction, and time spent with key page content
- Offer/category engagement: Relative interest across hotel, dining, and entertainment offers
- CTA intent: Clicks on Book Now, Check Rates, Reserve, Buy Tickets, Learn More, and outbound/vendor links
- Booking, reservation, and ticketing flow starts: Intent actions that may indicate movement toward conversion but do not prove completed outcomes
- Device differences: Mobile versus desktop behavior, especially engagement depth and CTA intent
- Third-party conversion visibility: Whether booking engine, SevenRooms, AXS, Fevo, or other vendor data can confirm completed bookings, reservations, ticket purchases, or revenue
- Tracking health: Whether GA4, GTM, Pendo, campaign UTMs, and vendor reporting are complete enough to support leadership conclusions
